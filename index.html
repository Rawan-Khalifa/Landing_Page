About Me

Hi! I'm Rawan, a Junoir at Minerva University, majoring in Computational Sciences.
My diverse academic and cultural experiences have shaped my commitment to global learning and interdisciplinary collaboration.
My mission is to leverage the power of technology and machine learning to solve complex challenges in healthcare and sustainability, while continuously exploring diverse cultures and perspectives around the world. I aim to integrate data-driven solutions and innovative AI approaches to enhance human well-being and advance global health outcomes.

Buenos Aires Highlight: Palace of Running Waters

A hidden architectural gem, the Palace of Running Waters is a testament to the grandeur of Buenos Aires’ history. It now serves as the city’s water management building, but its intricate façade and interior design showcase a bygone era of luxury and opulence.
As a part of our Minerva City Experience, I had the chance to attend a talk about Sustainability in Buenos Aires and discuss with leaders in companies like Aysa about the challanges and potential solutions.

SF Highlight: Foreshadowing a Deep Brain Stimulation Surgery at UCSF

Despite being a machine learning intern, I had the unique opportunity to foreshadow a surgery, talk with neurosurgeons, and translate this experience into impactful technical work.
In Prof. Morrison Melanie's Lab, I analyze preoperative and postoperative MRI images of Parkinson's Disease patients following Deep Brain Stimulation (DBS) surgery.
Currently, I am building a model to classify patient outcomes based on quantified metrics.
For more Lab information, check Neuromodulation Imaging Lab

South Korea Highlight: Mount Hallasn

“More passion..more energy..more footwork!”. My first hiking journey was an 11-hour hike to the highest point in South Korea!
It seemed impossible but WE made it!! It wasn't an individual triumph, but a collective effort and shared success. As a beginner, it seemed unbelievable even now but I learned that personal beliefs often impose the only limits. The lessons I learned from persevering through physical challenges will serve as a foundation for my journey into the realm of technological learning, reinforcing my commitment to overcoming obstacles, embracing teamwork, and achieving shared success.

    Check out our project Here

  • Decoding Relationships Between Genes

    This was one of the most meaningful Minerva assignmnets I have worked on. In this report I provide the implementation of the longest common subsequence problem in decoding the relationships between gene sequences. The goal is to recover the genealogy tree for a given set of strings represeting the gene sequences including the parent gene and the resulting genes from the replication of that gene and its children.
